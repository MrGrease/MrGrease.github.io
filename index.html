About Me

Ever since I learned Pascal in my 7th grade programming class in Sydney Australia I wanted to be a computer engineer...

Hi, I'm Emre and I'm a computer engineering graduate from Ankara, Turkey. I've spent most of my teenage years teaching myself how to code and worked on different projects ranging from video games to websites and animation. When I got into the Computer Engineering program at Başkent University in 2014, it felt like a continuation of my quest to unravel the mysteries of software development, rather than an entirely new experience.

With the guidance of my professors and my determined attitude I refined and mastered and the skills I needed to become a professional software engineer. My flexibility and ability to teach myself helped me overcome many of the challenges I faced, and they are still the two major strengths I posses that I still rely on today. Whether I'm building a new game for people to enjoy or creating an android application to speed up a tedious task I'm always looking for new secrets I can discover.

I've always worked on personal projects, even though I've released very few to the public, I've always loved the watching my ideas grow into something real. Throughout my University years I've worked on many video game projects one of which I released on the App-store and one of which I hope to release on Steam one day. Though I didn't limit myself to video games and have worked on many other types of projects including Android applications.

When I'm not working, I'm usually trying to find new spots to skate in, ever since my sudden introduction to surfing in Australia I've been extremely interested in anything that counts as a "board", if you can stand on it odds are I can skate it. I've also been a guitarist for a while and was lucky enough to play with a few bands and of course, I play a metric ton of video games which is what got me into computers in the first place. Currently I'm working on sharpening up my skills as I only recently finished my military service, and also looking for a team that can satisfy my desire to learn and grow.

So if you feel like you I'd make a good fit for your team feel free to get in touch.
